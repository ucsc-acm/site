Article I: Name and Purposes

1.1 Name

  1. This organization shall be called Student Chapter of the Association for Computing Machinery at University of California, Santa Cruz herein referred to as ACM at UCSC.
  2. This organization may officially be referred to by any of the following terms:
    1. Student Chapter of the Association for Computing Machinery at University of California, Santa Cruz
    2. Association for Computing Machinery at University of California, Santa Cruz
    3. ACM at UC Santa Cruz
    4. ACM at UCSC

1.2 Purposes

  1. ACM at UCSC seeks to promote interest in computer science and other science- and engineering-related disciplines. We aim to provide a strong sense of community to all engineering students within the Baskin School of Engineering, with a particular focus on students studying computer science.
  2. ACM at UCSC aims to host events in an effort to create an open and inclusive environment for all students from all majors to work together and network in a cohesive space.

Article II: Membership

2.1 Eligibility

  1. Membership in ACM at UCSC shall be open to all current and former students, staff, and faculty at the University of California, Santa Cruz.
  2. Membership shall not be restricted according to race, creed, age, gender, disability, nationality, sexual orientation, economic status, veteran status, or parenthood.

2.2 Classifications

  1. Member - Students, Staff or Faculty who completed registration of ACM at UCSC.
  2. Active Member - Members who attend at least 3 general meetings or events of ACM at UCSC in the past or current academic year.
  3. Alumni Member - Members who are no longer affiliated with the UCSC, members are automatically converted upon leaving the university.

2.3 Termination

  1. Membership in ACM at UCSC can be resigned at any time upon filing a written resignation to the chapter Secretary.
  2. Membership may be revoked from any member demonstrating behavior that is a substantial violation of the ACM Code of Conduct, University policies or campus regulations upon a three-fourths affirmative vote of Board of Directors.

Article III: Directors

3.1 Rules and Requirements

  1. The term of director is one academic year.
  2. No member may hold two positions simultaneously unless required by 3.2.3 of this Constitution.
  3. No member may be elected or appointed to the same position for three full consecutive terms unless there is no other qualified candidate.
  4. No more than three members may serve in Permanent Director Positions in their last year at UCSC unless there is no other qualified candidate.
  5. A member must serve on Board of Directors for a term before they can run for the positions of chairs.
  6. All directors serving in Permanent Director Positions must, upon election, become members of Association for Computing Machinery International.

3.2 Permanent Director Positions

  1. ACM at UCSC shall have six permanent director positions:
    1. Chair
    2. Vice Chair
    3. Secretary
    4. Treasurer
    5. Outreach Officer
    6. Webmaster
  2. A member may be elected to these positions according to the procedures specified in this Constitution.
  3. In case of one or more of Permanent Director Positions are not filled, The Board of Directors shall:
    1. Transfer the duties of the unfilled position to another director before a member can be appointed or elected to fill that position.
    2. Appoint a member to the unfilled position with the recommendation of Chair and consent of a minimum of two-third of Board of Directors membership; Or, by the petition of a minimum of one-third of Board of Directors membership, commerce a special election according to the procedures specified in this Constitution to elect a member to the unfilled position.

3.3 New Director Positions

  1. New Director Positions may be created with the consent of a minimum of two-third of Board of Directors membership.
  2. Titles of New Director Positions shall be specified in the Bylaws.
  3. A member may be appointed to these positions with the consent of a minimum of two-third of Board of Directors membership.
  4. A member may be elected to these positions according to the procedures specified in this Constitution.
  5. These positions shall be removed once they are vacated.
  6. No new director position may be created if one or more of Permanent Director Positions are not filled.

3.4 Emeritus Director Positions

  1. Upon election, up to two directors of former Board of Directors may choose to retain in new Board as Emeritus Director.
  2. Emeritus Directors shall retain their titles but with ”Emeritus” suffix.
  3. Former chairs shall get the priority. If they decline, former Board may vote to select Emeritus Director.

3.5 Board of Directors

  1. Board of Directors shall consist of all directors.
  2. Board of Directors shall meet when called by Chair or when called by a minimum of one-third of Board of Directors membership.
  3. Faculty adviser, a designated representative of each SIG and a designated representative of each committee may attend the meeting unless one-third of directors object.
  4. Board of Directors, with the consent of two-third majority of its membership, may invite other people to the meeting.
  5. Board of Directors voting rights extend only to directors.
  6. Board of Directors shall have following duties shared by all directors:
    1. Provides leadership and direction to ACM at UCSC;
    2. Understands and adheres to the Constitution of ACM at UCSC

3.6 Duties of Directors

  1. Chair
    1. Presides at meetings of the Chapter and oversees the activities of the board;
    2. Coordinates Chapter activities with the board members;
    3. Establishes short- and long-range objectives and goals in conjunction with the board members;
    4. Structures the organization to ensure continuity of leadership by providing opportunities for new leaders to develop and to be mentored;
    5. Involves all ACM members in the local chapter by planning activities that appeal to a wide spectrum of interests;
    6. Approves all Chapter communications;
    7. Ensures the completion of the Chapter annual report each year
  2. Vice Chair
    1. Presides at meetings in the absence of the Chair;
    2. Plans, coordinates, and recruits to manage a series of meetings and programs and activities;
    3. Coordinates programs with the Chair and the board members;
    4. Provides data on previous Chapter events to allow the event Chair to benefit from past experience and suggestions for improvement;
    5. Provides timely and interesting advance information for newsletters, social media, and mailings;
    6. Provides or coordinates information on forthcoming events to the Secretary for inclusion in meeting notices or newsletters;
    7. Ensures strong leadership succession by identifying and recruiting new Chapter volunteers;
    8. Provides mentorship to new officer(s)
  3. Secretary
    1. Handles the correspondence of the Chapter and keeps records of it;
    2. Maintains official records of meetings;
    3. Informs officers of deadlines for reports, mailings, future commitments;
    4. Organizes and oversees the elections according to the procedures specified in this Constitution.
    5. Works closely with Webmaster to maintain a roster of officers and other board members with current address, including email, and telephone information;
    6. Works closely with Outreach Officer to promote upcoming events and communicate with Chapter members;
    7. Assists Treasurer and Outreach Officer to prepare the documents for fundraising events;
    8. Maintains complete and up-to-date copies of the Chapter’s paperwork and other organizational documents
  4. Treasurer
    1. Oversees Chapter finances, collects dues, and receives other monies, e.g. proceeds from fundraisers;
    2. Follows best financial practices in consolidation with the board members;
    3. Completes and submits the annual financial report;
    4. Assists the Chair and other officers in preparing program budgets and financial controls;
    5. Coordinates activities and fundraisers with the board;
    6. Maintains and supervises Chapter bank accounts;
    7. Ensures that adequate budget and financial controls are maintained;
    8. Prepares and submits financial statements to the Chair and the board on a regular basis, i.e. all board meetings or at minimum quarterly;
    9. Works closely with Outreach Officer to organize and oversee the fundraising events of the Chapter;
    10. Pays all Chapter bills on time
  5. Outreach Officer
    1. Collaborates with other members of the board to create and execute exciting, interesting events for the Chapter constituency, e.g. Company Tours, professional events;
    2. Works closely with Treasurer to ensures the fiscal viability of all events;
    3. Works closely with Treasurer to organize and oversee the fundraising events of the Chapter;
    4. Works closely with Secretary to promote upcoming events and maintain records of correspondence;
    5. Gives alerts of upcoming events with details so the event may be advertised and marketed well;
    6. Publicizes Chapter activities through email, social media and the Chapter website
  6. Webmaster
    1. Keeps the organizations website up to date;
    2. Maintains Chapter history and list of past officers;
    3. Updates meeting time and location information on the website;
    4. Create/maintain website from that allows for new members to join the mailing list;
    5. Generates ideas regarding content and applies appropriate member ideas.
    6. Coordinates mailing of notices/newsletters to members and alumni;
    7. Works closely with Secretary and Outreach Officer to inform Chapter members of plans and activities by forwarding copies of all mailings and special reports;
    8. Works in coordination with BSOE webmaster to add any edits to the ACM website and/or give other and incoming board members access to edit webpage
  7. New Director Positions
    1. Duties of New Director Positions shall be specified in the Bylaws.
    2. Chair, with the advice and consent of a minimum of two-third of Board of Directors membership, may delegate duties of one or more Permanent Director Positions to New Director Positions.
  8. Emeritus Director Positions
    1. Emeritus Directors shall provide advice to Board of Directors.
    2. Emeritus Directors shall especially help directors who serve in former Board positions of Emeritus Directors.

Article IV: Public Representations and Relations

4.1 Association for Computing Machinery

  1. ACM at UCSC is affiliated with Association for Computing Machinery as a local student chapter.
  2. Authorized Representatives of ACM at UCSC to Association for Computing Machinery shall be:
    1. Chair
    2. Vice Chair
    3. Secretary
    4. Treasurer
  3. Authorized Representatives should comply with requirements of ACM to Authorized Representatives.
  4. In the event of the absense of an Authorized Representative, Board of Directors, with the consent of majority of its membership, may select another director as Authorized Representative.

4.2 University of California, Santa Cruz

  1. ACM at UCSC is affiliated with University of California, Santa Cruz and its units as an on-campus student organization.
  2. Authorized Representatives of ACM at UCSC to University of California, Santa Cruz shall be:
    1. Chair
    2. Vice Chair
    3. Treasurer
    4. Outreach Officer
  3. Authorized Representatives should comply with requirements of University of California, Santa Cruz to Authorized Representatives.
  4. In the event of the absense of an Authorized Representative, Board of Directors, with the consent of majority of its membership, may select another director as Authorized Representative.

Article V: Elections and Voting Procedures

  1. Any member who wishes to be considered for a position is eligible to be elected.
  2. Only active members are eligible to vote in elections.
  3. Member wishing to run shall give a speech vying for their position.
  4. Elections for positions will happen in the same order as 3.6 of this constitution.
  5. Elections should use ”Ranked-choice voting” which means the method of casting and tabulating votes in which voters rank candidates in order of preference, tabulation proceeds in sequential rounds in which last-place candidates are defeated and the candidate with the most votes in the final round is elected.

Article VI: Amendments

  1. Board of Directors, with the consent of two-third of its membership, can propose an amendment to this constitution.
  2. Board of Directors, with the consent of two-third of its membership, can propose and approve an amendment to Bylaws.
  3. All amendments to constitution must be approved by two-third of active members.
  4. Proposed amendments to constitution or bylaws must be submitted, in writing or electronically, to active members at least one week before formal consideration.

Article VII: Dissolution of the Chapter

  1. In the event of dissolution of this Chapter, all assets will be transferred to the national ACM organization unless the University of California requires that such assets be transferred to it for a purpose within the contemplation of section 501(c) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1954.
